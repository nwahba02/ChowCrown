# Product

## Register

brand+product (dual: brand pages expressive and personality-driven; product flow focused but on-brand)

## Users

Passionate local food diners in their 20s–40s who eat out regularly and want to settle arguments about what's actually the best. They're at the restaurant table, buzzing after a great meal, scanning a QR code on their phone. Also restaurant owners hungry to prove their dish beats the competition. Mobile is the primary device for the voting flow; desktop or mobile for browsing.

## Product Purpose

ChowCrown is a verified food competition platform. Only diners who physically eat at a restaurant can vote (enforced by QR scan). Live leaderboards update as votes come in. Winners are crowned each quarter. Success means diners feel like they're part of a real competition, not just leaving a review — and restaurants get trustworthy signal about how their food actually stacks up.

## Brand Personality

Celebratory · Electric · Community. The energy of a championship night meets the joy of eating amazing food together. Bold and competitive, but warm — this is a crowd cheering together, not a critic sitting alone.

## Anti-references

- Yelp / Google Reviews: utilitarian, anonymous, star ratings, no energy
- Food Network / recipe sites: lifestyle-magazine warmth, cooking-show photography, soft aesthetics

## Design Principles

1. Championship energy: Every screen should feel like an event is happening, not a form to fill out.
2. The crowd decides: Emphasize community, live updates, collective energy — the leaderboard is alive.
3. Bold over subtle: Typography at scale, color with conviction. No muted defaults, no safe choices.
4. Mobile-first visceral: Voting happens on a phone at the table — make it immediate, satisfying, and fun.
5. Earn trust visibly: The QR verification is the core differentiator — design it to feel secure AND exciting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. 44px minimum touch targets, accessible focus states, sufficient color contrast throughout.
